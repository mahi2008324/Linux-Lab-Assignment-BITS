System calls are the interface between user-space programs and the kernel. They include
operations like read(), write(), fork(), exec(), and exit() in Linux.

Answer 2:
The page replacement algorithm determines which page to swap out when memory is full.
LRU (Least Recently Used) is optimal in practice; OPT is theoretically optimal.

Answer 3:
Signals are software interrupts sent to processes. Common signals include SIGTERM for
graceful termination, SIGKILL for forced kill, and SIGCHLD for child status changes.
